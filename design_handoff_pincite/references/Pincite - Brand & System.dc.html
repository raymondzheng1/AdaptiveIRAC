Foundation

A study tool you can cite.

The whole product rests on one promise: grounded in your own materials, every citation pinpointed — or it declines, never invents. This system makes that trustworthiness visible. Calm, precise, scholarly — a quality research tool, deliberately not a generic novelty app.

North star
Earned trust. In ~5 seconds a sceptical law student should feel “this won’t make up cases.”
Feeling
Anxious → reassured → focused. Quiet authority under exam pressure.
Hard rule
No “AI” anywhere. No sparkles, robots, or magic framing. Credibility comes from rigour.
01 · The mark

The check as a seal of verification

A single deliberate checkmark, set in a rounded ink square. Read it as a stamp — checked against your materials — not a generic tick. No scales of justice. The mark is the same gesture the product makes on every citation.

02 · Name & wordmark

Four lockups to choose from

All law-literate, none “tech.” My pick is Pincite — a pinpoint citation is the exact page or paragraph a source supports. It is the product’s magic, and every law student knows the term. The name is a single token swap, so nothing here is locked.

Recommended
Pincite
A pinpoint citation — the exact source location. Crisp, ownable, instantly legible to its audience.
Headnote
The editor’s summary atop a reported case. Scholarly, structured, calm.
Marginalia
The notes in the margin of your own books. Warm, studious, a touch literary.
Adaptive IRAC
The working name — descriptive and safe. Keep it if you’d rather not rebrand.

My recommendation

A “Statute” as the base, with B’s mono citations. The Newsreader serif gives the scholarly authority that long IRAC prose needs and instantly separates us from generic apps; Hanken Grotesk keeps the editor and UI clean and warm; IBM Plex Mono on citation chips reads as precise and checkable. That hybrid is what the tokens and components below are built in.
